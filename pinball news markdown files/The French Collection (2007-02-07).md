Story  dated
February 7, 2007
.

Mention the words "pinball" and "museum" in the same sentence and your thoughts might turn to Tim Arnold's Pinball Hall of Fame in Las Vegas (which Pinball News will be visiting shortly for a full report).

But the city in the desert is now joined  as home to a superb collection of classic pinball games by Paris.

This is the Pinball Museum located in Aubervilliers, just north of Paris in France and although not yet open to the general public, the collection can be viewed by appointment with the Museum's owner Raphael Lankar who welcomes visitors to his creation.

Raphael told Pinball News how he has been working towards this museum since the 1950's and has been getting the 200 pinball and 50 slot machine exhibits together over the past three years.

There are also several jukeboxes and an extensive collection of backglasses on display around the museum building.

The majority of the collection cover the years 1931-1975 with 90% being of American origin. It includes some rare European conversions of well known US games alongside some genuine classics from the era. All the games have been restored to the highest quality possible as you can see from these pictures.

It hasn't been easy either. Raphael said making his dream a reality took "a lot of money, a lot of people,  a lot of organization and a lot of me."

But his search for games hasn't finished with the opening of the museum. He is still on the look out for  rarer games and examples which are closer to perfection to compliment the existing collection.

Although it is intended to eventually open the doors for general admission, until then visitors wishing to experience this superb collection for themselves should contact the museum's owner at raphael-lankar@wanadoo.fr

You can get more details at the museum's website pinball-gallery.com where you can also view images of each machine in the collection and all the backglasses.
